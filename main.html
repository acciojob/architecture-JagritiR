Architectural Information

Architecture is the art and science of designing and constructing buildings and other physical structures. Architecture can be classified into different styles, such as classical, Gothic, Renaissance, modern, and postmodern. Some of the factors that influence architectural design are culture, history, climate, function, and aesthetics.

The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater in Rome, Italy. It was built in the first century AD by the Roman emperors Vespasian and Titus. It could hold up to 80,000 spectators who watched gladiator fights, animal hunts, and other public events. The Colosseum is one of the most iconic examples of Roman architecture and engineering.
The Eiffel Tower is a wrought iron lattice tower in Paris, France. It was built in 1889 by Gustave Eiffel for the World's Fair. It stands at 324 meters tall and is the most visited paid monument in the world. The Eiffel Tower is a symbol of French culture and innovation.
